The Pirate Game

This game celebrates and introduces Quidco's new branding. The squids are a relatively new brand element and the game was designed to expolore that plaufylness and get customers to actively interact with it.

Our UX strategy consisted of growing customer loyalty and we drew up some experience maps from the user research for first time users and new users. A majority of our findings concluded that customers did not feel particularly loyal to the brand and their engagement was quite minimal on a daily basis.

We found that the game tripled active members daily and with the help of the social team we found that customers identified with the different squid personalities we created and introduced a more human, emotional aspect to the brand.

The obstacles

We wanted to run a game on special occasions across the year, so decided to build a template that can be reused and re-themed each time. There was a small obstacle in planning our the libraries and components to make sure they can work across multiple devices and be prepared for the CMS to be automated. What really helped was documenting how components behave to make sure the development and the design teams would be on the same page in the future.

The process

The game was designed and built for Android, iOS and Web. We agreed on the theme with the team and I worked on bringing that storyline to the UI. The squids were designed in Illustrator and little instances of animation have been made in After Effects, ready to be exported to Lottie. The storytelling aspect to this game was really fun to work on, especially making sure that each step and piee of UI tells a story and everything is connected.
