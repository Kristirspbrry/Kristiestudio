Intro

This was a redesign and a UX exercise, putting the focus on our customers needs first. I put together the UX architecture and examined user tests with experience maps so that we could find and fix any pitfalls and learn what the users are looking for.

The challenge

For this project I was utilising the data we had previously collected from live user tests, which I collated into experience maps to outline customer needs, issues, opportunities and pitfalls.

I put together some flows to outline all the possible journeys a customer could take to get to their destination.

These metrics were used a foundation to redesign the homepage and to deliver a customer focused user experience

The process and ideas

Taking account all of the data from experience maps, I started experimenting with some ideas to combat the pitfalls that have been identified.

Here, I wanted to explore and experiment with how we could convey that in a way that makes more sense. One of the ideas was to introduce Quidco as a digital loyalty card - always remembering to use Quidco when shopping online - just like getting out a loyalty card when at a shop, as that is already a widely used and understood concept.

Also, making their homepage experience a little more personal - adding recent activity, deals from their favourite merchants, as well as offering easier access to categories and curating the content. Here I wanted to explore layout and UI that would reflect the brand. We discovered from user testing that customers have shown an interest in articles, especially relating to financial information or gift sets. So the homepage would not just a list of brands that need to be promoted, but also a source of potentially useful information for the user, as well as giving them the ability to look through categories themselves.
