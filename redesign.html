Intro

Streetbees were a data start-up when I joined this project. We were building an app to collect data from hard to reach countries and people, which then was translated to a dashboard for various companies we partnered with. Some of the clients included Nestle, Dyson, Unilever.

The challenge

The challenge here was to really understand the goals the clients were after and how the data needed to be displayed. Navigation played a really important part. We built a general template but each survey needed to be individually designed and prepared to some extent, so when working on layouts, we needed to consider various scenarios.

It was important that the navigation had a flow from beginning to end that told a story about the research, and it was important for the brands (our other users) to be able to dissect and search the information in whatever way they needed, so via tags and filters, getting in to the small details of each project.

To achieve that we looked at flows within navigation and also how we can dissect information in each step.

The objective

The objective was to engage users to take part in survey feeds so we can collect their data via an app, which then would be translated as data in a dashboard for brands to access.

The deliverables were a mobile app for the users to take surveys and a web app dashboard for brands to access the collected data.

Solving problems

We wanted to find the best way for users to engage with our content. We looked at similar concepts such as Facebook / Instagram where blocks of information are displayed in a scroll format as a trend of interaction just so we could find a common UX pattern that would be widely recognised for our worldwide audience.

It was important for the business to keep users engaged and keep them coming back. One of the ideas we explored in achieving that was through engaging users in local news. The Streetbees concept was all about real users getting a say about their experience of a product so we wanted to keep within those boundaries, but push what opinions they could express further into local news and other areas of life.

The process

We were able to test the app with a variety of users and we were working with B2B clients in order to achieve their dashboards goals. The idea was that we would create data display blueprints for the future, so the work would not need to be manual.

I worked in a squad with web app and app developers and 2 Lead designers. I was able to work on some front-end projects while at Streetbees as well.
