Overview

  • 1 week Design sprint

Intro

This design sprint was based on testing an assumption that getting users to open up the app will increase their engagement with the content.

The business also wanted to give back to their customers over the festive period.

We built an interactive game with a time frame of 1 month from start to shipping to explore how this theory may work.

I followed a 5 day design sprint framework. I then spent more time over the month working on animations and working in an agile way with the team.

Day 1

I set up a workshop to ideate on what kind of product we could build. Looking at other brands and ideas around Christmas we settled on a game. I spent the rest of the day sketching ideas and researching.

Day 2

I put together wireframes to test the UX and some low-fi visuals for the game UI to get it ready for prototyping for the next day.

Day 3

I put together the flows and the artwork, considering the feedback and amendments that other team memebers offered from looking at the wireframes on day 2 and started to prototype.

Day 4

Working on the prototype and also beginning to plan animations and how the interactive elements can work and behave.

Day 5

Handing off the prototype to developers and continuing work on animation. The animations took a little longer as we had to search to find a quick solution for the devs, so we settled with Lottie and I was animating on After Effects so the animations could be exported as xml files.

Testing and feedback

For this project, we were testing our assumption from the data we received once the game was live. We found that there was a significant increase in users opening the app once the game went live (up by 50%). However, there didn‚Äôt seem to be a significant increase in engagement with other content on the app.

Failed sprint? Conclusion

So, our assumptions were sort of correct, however, we deduced it did not bring any real value to the business long term. So, the real problem that we could see now is that we need to possibly reconsider the way the content is presented on the app and test that hypothesis.
