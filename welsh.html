Intro

This project started with a problem I aimed to solve for myself - how to learn a language in a quick and efficient way.

There is a common notion that learning some languages is difficult. It can be difficult and time consuming to understand the grammar and remember certain structures. One common issue I discovered through my research is that most of the language learning apps and online tools aren‚Äôt very good at explaining how something works in great detail. Or there is hardly any 'soul' of the language in the material. You may also have to learn an array of basics that aren‚Äôt immediately useful.

This product encapsulates my needs as a learner, as well as the needs of the Welsh community and those new to Learning Welsh.

It was also published and featured on the UX Collective. Read it here

Research and challenges

I studied and researched several sources for Welsh lessons, both adult and children to see if there is much of a difference. I found that a lot of material for adult learning is very black and white and it takes a lot of concentration in order to learn and understand. Children‚Äôs lessons seemed a lot more engaging, however. I found that most sources also only taught the southern Welsh language, even though the language does differ quite a bit across the country.

Proposed Solutions

As language is so multidimensional, it made sense to add that aspect to designing a language course. There is a neurological condition called synesthesia, when you may see shapes when you hear music or see colours with sounds. I borrowed this concept for this project.

So to highlight a sound I used a colour and all native Welsh sounds in the alphabet are coloured. This course is primarily aimed at English language speakers, so the rest of the letters that are pronounced the same as in English are a neutral colour.

Community testing and revisions

This project undergone a great number of revisions. Both from testing with native speakers and the friends and family I could grab. I really enjoyed being able to work with such a huge variety of people.

Pretty much all language courses focus on teaching the dialect that is most widely used or most widely accepted in literature. However, while running tests with my product, it was clear that there is quite a significant language diversity between certain regions in Wales. Some words are totally different! This tends to create a divide and I really wanted my product to be accessible and enjoyed by anyone in the country (someone did say that he couldn‚Äôt find anything that was north Wallian which was putting him off the course). So, to solve that issue I consulted people from across the country to record their pronunciations and update some of the grammar and words.

Final product

The final product was a course that encapsulated the Welsh language in succinct and fun 10 day classes. It outlines the most important grammatical points, language variations across the country with recorded pronunciations by the locals and builds up knowledge through progressive learning. The last lesson emulates a test, however, in this case it‚Äôs about being able to apply what‚Äôs been learned throughout the classes and the learners can test themselves if they have understood and remembered what they‚Äôve learned in the previous 9 lessons.

I built a web page for this course, as it‚Äôs an easy access for anyone. I designed the content (which included me needing to learn Welsh!), designed the layout and coded it all up. I did get a lot of help from the native Welsh speakers to make sure the grammar is correct. So essentially this product has been built by me and the Welsh community.

I wanted to structure this in such a way that made it seem like it was easily digestable and not overwhelming. When I was learning and reading through the grammar I didn't have the easiest of time connecting all the dots.

Target audience

I designed this course to be accessible to children and adults alike - anyone could pick it up. A foreign language can seem shrouded in myth and unfamiliarity, so my aim was to make it as human and fun as possible. The last lesson of the course encapsulates all the lessons and invites the learner to improvise and write a story (about a dragon of course), as a sort of end of course test, however, I aimed to make that the most fun part of the learning.

Keeping this in mind, the font face I chose was Manrope. The font in this case was important to me that it could convey the message, be clear and encompass my target audience.

I received some incredible feedback from people all across the internet on this project. Some offered to pay for the classes, which I would conclude signified a successful outcome.
